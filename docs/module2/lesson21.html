Lesson 2.1: Match-3 Games

Got puzzle?
The final game for this course is a match-3 puzzle game. However, you can use the techniques and code samples to make a huge variety of puzzle games. The secret to making a good game is to build on existing ideas, adding your own creative spin.

Match-3 games present the player with a grid of multicolored tiles. The player swaps two tiles next to each other trying to make matches of three or more like tiles in a row. There are many variations of match-3 (also known as tile-matching) games. Three classic examples are Shariki, Bejeweled, and Candy Crush Saga.

In 1994 Russian programmer Eugene Alemzhin created the game Shariki (which translates to “The Balls”). In this game, players are presented with a grid of various colored balls. The object of the game was to swap adjacent balls to make a row of three like-colored balls. These balls would explode and new ones would appear in their place. Players would try to get as high a score as possible before there were no more moves available - marking the end of the game.

Bejeweled, first released by PopCap Games, was a gorgeous reinvention of the basic match-3 game. Instead of different colored balls, players would try to match gems - each color gem had it’s own unique shape. Bonus points and abilities are given for cascades (when the gems that fill in the cleared spaces also make matches) and for matches of 4 or more gems. A bar at the bottom of the game grid fills with each match made, and the player advances to the next level when the bar is completely filled. The game board is reset at the start of a level, and the game is over when no more moves are available.

In 2012, the casual games developer King took the world by storm when it released Candy Crush Saga, a Facebook and smartphone variation of their Candy Crush web browser game. Players progress through levels matching different colored and shaped candies. Like Bejeweled, Candy Crush Saga grants additional points and special abilities for cascade matches or for making matches of four or more candies. Levels can also have additional challenges the player must meet, such as clearing specific tiles within a certain amount of moves.

If you want inspiration on how good a match-3 game can be, you should check out one of the more recent versions of Bejeweled or Candy Crush Saga.
